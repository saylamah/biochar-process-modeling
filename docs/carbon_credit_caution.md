# Carbon-credit and carbon-management caution

This repository uses the expression **preliminary carbon-credit potential** only as a screening-level engineering concept.

Formal carbon-credit eligibility requires more than biochar production. It normally requires a defined methodology, eligible and sustainable biomass sourcing, complete system boundaries, durable carbon-storage evidence, baseline comparison, leakage assessment, monitoring, reporting, verification, traceability, and independent certification.

For this repository, the responsible language is:

- carbon-management potential
- screening-level carbon-accounting logic
- preliminary carbon-credit potential
- durable-carbon-storage discussion
- process-emission comparison

Avoid unsupported language such as:

- certified carbon credits
- guaranteed carbon neutrality
- zero-emission process, unless the full boundary is explicitly defined
- investment-grade carbon-removal calculation

The three-case comparison is useful for technical orientation: full syngas combustion has high direct CO2 and NOx emissions; partial syngas combustion reduces direct emissions and preserves syngas value; non-oxidizing renewable hot-gas treatment has the strongest screening-level potential if renewable heat supply, process integration, product use, and carbon permanence are verified.
